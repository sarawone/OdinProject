Mohinga

Mohinga is the national dish of Myanmar (Burma), consisting of a fragrant, savory fish-based broth served over rice vermicelli noodles. It is widely considered a breakfast staple but is enjoyed throughout the day as a popular street food and comfort meal.

Ingredients

  • Fish & Stock: Typically catfish (fresh) or tinned pilchards/sardines/mackerel in brine.
  • Aromatics (Paste Base): Lemongrass (stalks), ginger, garlic, shallots (or onions), and turmeric powder.
  • Thickener: Toasted rice flour or gram (chickpea) flour.
  • Flavorings: Fish sauce, shrimp paste, and sometimes chilli powder/paprika.
  • Texture: Sliced banana stem (or lotus root).
  • Noodles: Thin rice vermicelli.
  • Hard-boiled eggs
  • Crispy fried onions or garlic
  • Crispy chana dal crackers or fritters
  • Fresh coriander
  • Lime wedges
  • Dried chilli flakes or oil

Steps Instructions

1. Prepare The Fish & Broth

  1. Boil the fish: In a large pot, combine the fish with 3 smashed lemongrass stalks, 1 tsp turmeric, 2 tbsp fish sauce, and 6 cups of water. Boil for 15–20 minutes until the fish is cooked through.
  2. Debone: Remove the fish and let it cool. Carefully flake the flesh, ensuring all bones are removed.
  3. Save the Stock: Strain the liquid, keeping the broth. Set both the fish and stock aside.

2. Prepare the Flavor Base (Aromatics)

  1. Make Paste: Pound or blend the ginger, garlic, shallots, and the inner white parts of the remaining lemongrass into a fine paste.
  2. Fry Paste: Heat 3-4 tbsp of oil in a pot over medium heat. Add the pounded paste, paprika, and turmeric. Fry for 5–7 minutes until fragrant and the oil separates.
  3. Add Fish: Add the flaked fish to the paste mixture and stir-fry for another 5 minutes to infuse the flavor.

3. Assemble and Simmer the Soup

  1. Combine: Pour the reserved fish stock back into the pot with the fish and paste mixture.
  2. Thicken: In a small bowl, mix the toasted rice powder and chickpea flour with a little cold water to make a smooth slurry. Stir this into the soup to avoid clumping.
  3. Simmer: Add banana stem (if using) and extra fish sauce/salt to taste. Simmer for 15–30 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.

4.Prepare Noodles and Serving

  1. Noodles: Soak dried rice vermicelli in hot water for 10–15 minutes until soft, then drain.
  2. Serve: Place a portion of noodles in a bowl, ladle the hot soup over them, and garnish with sliced boiled eggs, coriander, lime, fried shallots, and chili
