Shan Noodle

Shan Noodles (Shan Khao Swe) is a hallmark of Burmese cuisine, originating from the Shan State in eastern Myanmar. It is a versatile dish typically consisting of thin or flat rice noodles topped with a rich, tomato-based chicken or pork meat sauce and a variety of flavorful garnishes.

Ingredients

  • Flat Rice Noodle
  • Protein & Sauce: Ground chicken or pork simmered with chopped tomatoes, garlic, onion, ginger, turmeric powder, paprika (or chilli powder), fish sauce, and light soy sauce
  • Cooking Oil: Peanut or vegetable oil, sometimes infused with toasted sesame oil.
  • Crispy fried garlic and garlic oil
  • Thinly sliced spring onions
  • Chilli flakes or chili oil for heat.
  • Lime wedges

Steps Instructions

1. Prepare The Meat Sauce

  1. Heat 2-3 tablespoons of oil in a wok or large pan over medium heat.
  2. Add finely chopped shallots and garlic, sauté until fragrant and slightly browned.
  3. Add paprika/chili powder, turmeric, and Chinese five-spice powder. Stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the ground chicken or pork, stirring to break up the meat, and cook until it starts to brown.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes, soy sauce, and salt.
  6. Add about ½ cup of water, cover the pan, and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the tomatoes have softened into a thick sauce and the meat is cooked through.

2. Prepare The Noodle

  1. Boil the rice noodles according to the package instructions.
  2. Once cooked, strain and rinse immediately with cold water to prevent sticking.

3. Prepare the Garnish/Sides

  1. Garlic Oil: Fry chopped garlic in oil until golden brown.
  2. Peanuts: Roast and crush peanuts.
  3. Vegetables: Slice spring onions and prepare fresh coriander

4. Assemble and Serve

  1. Place a portion of noodles into a serving bowl
  2. Top with 2-3 tablespoons of the chicken/pork tomato sauce.
  3. Add 1 tablespoon of garlic oil, a dash of light soy sauce, and chilli oil to taste.
  4. Garnish with crushed peanuts, coriander, spring onions, and pickled mustard greens.
  5. Mix well before eating.
